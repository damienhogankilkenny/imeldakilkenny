Imelda Norris Studio

Even if you know where your pelvic floor is, would you know how to use it? Well Madonna, Gwyneth Paltrow and Julia Roberts do. Kilkenny is fast becoming the fitness capital of Ireland and Imelda Norris is at the forefront of the latest addition - Pilates, which she has been teaching for the last 13 years, here in Kilkenny.Imelda says the reasons people come to Pilates vary from bad posture, weak backs, wanting to pull in stomachs after having babies, more and more sports people use it to improve their game,she says.

In her experience people in Kilkenny are very conscious of their fitness. She also has a huge uptake in her evening yoga classes, so much so that she will also be giving morning and lunchtime classes in both exercises next September, to keep up with the demand.

Imelda is unusual in that she teaches both Pilates and Yoga and she points out that a lot of Pilates exercises are actually similar to, or derived from Yoga. Her strong Yoga background obviously gives her an edge when teaching the newer Pilates, which was developed in 1920s.

"People in Kilkenny are always looking for something new, so I will also be introducing a power yoga class.

She explains "the reason Pilates and Yoga work so well for celebrities is that they both stretch and strengthen in the same movement which most other exercises don not do. This tones and strengthens but also gives a nice lean and flexible body. But it is like anything, you have to practice. You cant expect a one hour class to do miracles. But even simple things like keeping your pelvic floor and stomach pulled in slightly while doing the washing up or driving, for instance, can make a difference."
